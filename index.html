NJ HOT Lanes Revenue EstimatorNEW

How much could dynamic toll pricing on New Jersey's express lane corridors generate for NJ Transit? Adjust assumptions across six corridors — NJ Turnpike, I-78, I-80, and three Garden State Parkway segments — including AADT, opt-in rates, average dynamic toll, and weekday/weekend parameters. LIHTC Credit CalculatorNEW

Estimate annual and 10-year tax credit equity from a LIHTC project — toggle between 9% and 4% credit types, adjust total development cost, ineligible cost share, and affordable unit percentage. Shows the eligible basis waterfall. Housing Policy Series Part III.

LIHTC Crowd-Out: Supply & Demand IncidenceNEW

Interactive supply-and-demand diagram showing how supply and demand elasticity determine whether LIHTC construction adds net housing units or displaces private market production. Presets for Sinai–Waldfogel, Eriksen–Rosenthal, and Baum-Snow–Marion estimates. Housing Policy Series Part III.

Diamond & McQuade (2019): Spillover EffectsNEW

Radial diagram showing property value spillover effects within 0.1, 0.25, and 0.5 miles of a LIHTC development — +6.5% in low-income neighborhoods vs. −2.5% in higher-income neighborhoods. Click rings to explore each distance band. Housing Policy Series Part III.

LIHTC Equity Pricing: Cents per Dollar of CreditNEW

Historical chart of LIHTC equity pricing from 2008 to 2025, with annotated events: Great Recession collapse (~$0.62), 2016 peak ($1.05), 2017 TCJA shock, and the 2025 One Big Beautiful Bill. Housing Policy Series Part III.

LIHTC Set-Aside ElectionsNEW

Interactive unit grid for comparing the 20/50, 40/60, and Average Income Test set-aside elections. Click units to designate AMI levels (20–80% AMI) and the compliance checker tracks whether the project meets the minimum set-aside and, for the AIT, the 60% AMI average cap. Housing Policy Series Part III.

Do You Qualify for Affordable Housing in Jersey City?NEW

Enter household size and income to see your AMI tier (very-low / low / moderate / above), where you fall on the AMI scale, and the maximum UHAC rent at your tier by bedroom size. Region 1 (Bergen, Hudson, Passaic, Sussex) 2025 limits. Housing Policy Series Part II.

How UHAC Splits Affordable Units in a New BuildingNEW

Adjustable building size (up to 1,500 units) and set-aside percentage to visualize the 13% very-low / 37% low / 50% moderate income mix and the 80% 2-3BR / ≥20% 3BR bedroom mix that UHAC requires. Housing Policy Series Part II.

Where Does a Housing Voucher Actually Go?NEW

Interactive supply-and-demand model showing how housing supply elasticity determines whether a voucher creates new housing or gets captured as higher rent. In supply-constrained markets like Jersey City, most of the subsidy flows to landlords. Housing Policy Series Part II.

How Moving Chains Work

Step-through animation showing how new market-rate construction triggers a chain of moves that frees up affordable units across the market — with research callouts from Asquith, Pennington, and Bratu et al. Housing Policy Series Part I.

The Evidence: New Housing Lowers Rents

Filterable card explorer summarizing peer-reviewed findings on how new housing supply affects rents, displacement, and moving chains — covering studies from 12+ cities including NYC, San Francisco, Helsinki, and Jersey City. Housing Policy Series Part I.

The Fiscal Cost of Inclusionary Zoning in Jersey City

How unfunded, partially funded, and fully funded IZO mandates affect housing supply, affordable unit production, and property tax revenue for the city, schools, and Hudson County — with adjustable set-aside rate, density bonus, and PILOT rate sliders. Housing Policy Series.
