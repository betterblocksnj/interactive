Sources and methodology: AADT and peak-hour volume defaults: NJ Turnpike inner roadway (Exits 6–14) from Steer Davies Gleave / CRA International, New Jersey Traffic and Revenue Study — NJ Turnpike Asset Appraisal (January 2008), prepared for NJ Treasury; peak-hour counts at Exit 14C segment grown at ~1%/yr through 2019 with post-Covid normalization. Garden State Parkway corridors from Steer Davies Gleave / CRA International, New Jersey Traffic and Revenue Study — Garden State Parkway Asset Appraisal (January 2008), same study; AM peak-hour directional volumes and Level of Service analysis from Tables 2.8–2.9 (p. 25–26): Essex–Bergen at 87% of LOS D service volume is the most congested GSP segment; Raritan–Union at 71% with northbound speeds of 51.7 mph. GSP express/local infrastructure (Exits 102–125) confirmed in Section 2.10. I-78 and I-80 from NJDOT segment counts (2024 Traffic Viewer). 365-day tolling with separate weekday and weekend parameters. Weekday peak assumes 8–10% of AADT per hour at full dynamic toll rates. Off-peak applies reduced toll and opt-in rates. Weekend models lower volumes with distinct peak-hour patterns (e.g., midday shore traffic on GSP South, Sunday evening returns). Express lane opt-in rates benchmarked to Virginia I-66 and I-495 systems (20–30%). Average tolls from Virginia data ($5–$8 avg, peaks above $30). Corridors tagged "physically separated" have existing express/local infrastructure. "Lane designation" corridors require restriping 1–2 GP lanes as HOT. Pull segment-level AADT from NJDOT's Traffic Viewer for precise local inputs.
